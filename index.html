About

Lluka holds a Bachelor of Science in Palaeontology & Geology and a First-Class Honours Degree, both from the University of Adelaide. Their honours thesis described the enigmatic alkaline rock, nundorite, enriched in Zr, Nb and REE. After graduating, Lluka gained industry experience in mining and exploration, where they worked across multiple commodities and a range of geological settings. Through their work and studies Lluka has developed their skills in petrography, field work and geochemistry. Their background has given them a well-rounded perspective on geology and balancing the needs of research and industry. Lluka is currently a PhD student at ANU, collaborating with CSIRO and Lynas Rare Earth on their project on carbonatite hosted Nb-Ta. Lluka likes all things weird and wonderful about rocks, but they are particularly fond of alkaline-silicate systems and carbonatites. Their favourite research activites are fieldwork and microscope time. Lluka directs their passion into meaningful and practical research. They are always keen to talk geology, whether that be learning something new or sharing their own knowledge with anyone who wants to listen.



Current Research

Nb-Ta mineralisation of the Mt Weld Carbonatite

The Mt Weld carbonatite, Western Australia, is world renowned for its REE resources, however it also hosts other critical metals which are currently under researched and unexploited, including Nb and Ta. In order to understand the genesis and development of these metals, Lluka will characterise the mineralisation using petrographic and geochemical techniques. They will examine the mineralisation in both the hardrock and overlying supergene material, allowing them to pick apart the magmatic/hydrothermal history as well as supergene alteration and ore formation processes. This will advance understanding of the key geological controls governing carbonatite hosted Nb-Ta mineralisation, with implications for future explotation and economic development.


Past Projects


This enigmatic rock is know from only a single outcrop in northwestern NSW. Since its discovery in the 70's nundorite has been sold as a decorative stone with abundant metaphysical properties. Of more interest, is its anomolas geochemical composition. Nundorite is peralkaline with low silica and high alumina, yet it is enriched in Zr, Nb and REE, all of which are considered critial minerals.

This project involved comprahensive mineralogical and geochemical analysis, with the aim of unravelling the origins of nundorite, and further understanding geological processes that lead to rare metal concentration in the crust.

Johns-Mead, L.Y., Spandler, C., & Lloyd, J.C. (2024) Nundorite: a geological oddity of critical minerals significance from the Mount Arrowsmith Volcanics, northwestern NSW. Australian Journal of Earth Sciences, 71(8), 1223–1238. DOI: 10.1080/08120099.2024.2335970J
